Bioinformatics Visualization

Nineteen interactive explanations of the visualization techniques bioinformaticians use to make sense of multidimensional experiments. A two-gene detective story: TP53 is mutated, CDKN1A is affected, and every chart reveals another dimension of the evidence.

Modern biology generates enormous datasets: thousands of genes measured across dozens of conditions, millions of individual cells profiled one by one, entire genomes compared for structural rearrangements. Bioinformaticians have developed a distinctive visual vocabulary to navigate this complexity.

The first seven explanations introduce that vocabulary. The next twelve follow a single biological story: the tumor suppressor TP53 carries the R175H mutation, disrupting its ability to activate the cell cycle regulator CDKN1A. Each visualization adds a new dimension to the investigation, from single-cell expression to protein interactions to clinical outcomes.

Part of the moonshine project. All data is synthetic, generated in-browser.
