Parallel Coordinates: Geometry Beyond Sight

Twenty-nine interactive explorations of Inselberg's geometric framework for parallel coordinates. From the duality that makes it all work, through applied domains and exceptional root systems, to a final synthesis of the connections that emerged along the way.

A point in five-dimensional space is impossible to picture. But draw five parallel axes and connect the values with a polyline, and the point becomes visible. More than visible: the geometric relationships between dimensions become readable. Correlations appear as crossing patterns. Clusters emerge as bundles of lines. Hyperplanes leave structured signatures.

Alfred Inselberg spent decades developing the mathematical foundations that explain why this works. These explorations make that geometry interactive. Drag points, brush axes, rotate polytopes, and build intuition for structures that exist beyond three dimensions.
